Red Flags I Catch

Real issues found in recent engagements

‚ö†

Claim Mismatch

What was presented to investors as a decentralized, trust-minimized crypto-exchange was in fact centralized and high-risk. The inherent centralization of the platform meant that any of the employees of the company could abscond with the entire vault funds.

‚ö†

Technical Debt

Core parts of the system were written in an obsolete and highly esoteric programming language that only the CTO of the company could read. It implemented a proprietary MPC algorithm that was not documented anywhere and represented the most business-critical path in the system. This was recognized as being high risk both technically and from a knowledge-management point of view, and fixed by the CTO team before closing the round.

‚ö†

Scalability and Cost

A trading system was built using inadequate data architecture that was scaled up by running more AWS instances. We calculated that as the user-base grows, so will the cost of the infrastructure in such a way that revenue will go down sharply. The team came up with a plan to refactor the architecture so that scaling would be sub-linear in cost, thus allowing growth without limiting revenue.

‚ö†

Hidden Dependency

A large marketplace system was built using a third-party authentication provider. Analysis of the commercial agreement revealed that as the company's userbase rose, so would the cost of the third party integration, to the point that the product would no longer be profitable. We required the team to migrate to an open source solution, thus minimizing costs and allowing for unlimited growth.
